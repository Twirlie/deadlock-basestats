**Deadlock** is a third-person shooter and MOBA (multiplayer online battle arena) game developed by Valve, featuring two teams of six players competing to destroy the enemy "patron" — an effigy of an elder god set in New York.

Matches take place across a large multi-lane map, where players control unique heroes and advance AI-controlled "troopers" toward the enemy base. The game blends third-person shooter mechanics with deep MOBA elements like lane control, neutral monsters, and objectives — set against a fantastical neo-noir backdrop where the supernatural has become an undeniable reality.

The game features a fantasy/steampunk aesthetic with magicians, strange creatures, and robots, and has been compared to a mashup of Dota 2, Team Fortress 2, and Overwatch.

Currently still in early development, access to Deadlock is limited to friend invites from existing playtesters. Valve has been pushing out near-weekly updates covering character balancing, new heroes, and major gameplay changes, with a full release not expected before late 2026.

---

**The Three Core Stat Categories**

Every hero's stats — and every item in the shop — fall into one of three categories:

- **Weapon** — governs gun performance: Bullet Damage, DPS, Ammo, Bullets per Second, Fire Rate, and Bullet Lifesteal.
- **Vitality** — governs survivability: Max Health, Health Regen, Bullet Resist, Spirit Resist, Move Speed, Sprint Speed, and Stamina.
- **Spirit** — governs ability effectiveness. Spirit Power is the primary Spirit stat — Valve describes it as increasing "the effectiveness of your Abilities and Items," though it also boosts the base stats of specific heroes in unique ways. For example, Grey Talon's Bullet Damage and Move Speed scale with Spirit Power, while Haze's Spirit Power increases her magazine size (Ammo).

---

**Souls & Scaling**

As you collect more Souls throughout a match, your Base Health, Bullet Damage, and Melee Damage all increase. Melee Damage scales identically across all heroes, while Base Health and Bullet Damage scale differently per hero.

---

**Resists & Shields**

A Resist stat reduces a flat percentage of incoming damage of that type — for example, 20% Bullet Resist means 20% less damage from Bullet Damage. Shields, meanwhile, give a separate HP pool against a specific damage type. Once a shield's HP hits zero, it goes on a 60-second cooldown before regenerating near-instantly.

---

**Ability Points & Upgrades**

Each hero has three main abilities and an ultimate, all upgradeable using Ability Points earned by collecting Souls or completing objectives. The first upgrade costs 1 AP, the second costs 2 AP, and the final upgrade costs 5 AP.

---

**Item Slots**

Each hero has four item slots per category (Weapon, Vitality, Spirit), plus up to four Flex slots that accept items from any category — unlocked progressively by destroying enemy Guardians, Walkers, and Shrines. With all slots unlocked, a hero can equip up to 16 items total.
